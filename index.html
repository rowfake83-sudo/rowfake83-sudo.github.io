AI companies should follow clear rules before their systems affect real people.

Main Rules of the Law

  1. AI content labels: AI-generated images, videos, voices, and articles must be clearly labeled.
  2. User warning: Companies must tell users when they are talking to an AI chatbot instead of a real person.
  3. Safety testing: AI used in schools, jobs, healthcare, banking, or law enforcement must be tested before release.
  4. Bias checks: Companies must check if their AI treats people unfairly based on race, gender, age, language, income, or disability.
  5. Data rights: People must be told if their personal data is being used to train AI.
  6. Human review: If AI makes an important decision, the person affected should be able to ask for a human review.
  7. Penalties: Companies that break the law can receive fines or lose permission to use unsafe AI systems.

Students, workers, voters, families, and companies can all benefit from safer AI rules.

Who Benefits?

Students

Students benefit because AI misinformation becomes easier to identify. Schools can also use AI more fairly and safely.

Workers

Workers benefit because AI hiring systems must be checked for bias before being used to accept or reject applicants.

Voters

Voters benefit because fake political videos, voices, and AI-generated propaganda must be labeled clearly.

Consumers

Consumers benefit because companies must be honest about when AI is being used to sell products or make decisions.

Honest businesses also benefit because clear rules make the AI industry more trustworthy. Instead of banning AI, this law creates a fair system where companies can still innovate while protecting the public.

Some people may worry that AI regulation could slow down business or innovation.

Possible Criticisms

Some critics may argue that this law could slow down AI innovation. They may say that companies need freedom to experiment and release new technology quickly. Others may say that small businesses could struggle to pay for safety testing or legal requirements.

These concerns are important, but they do not mean AI should have no rules. The law could give small companies extra time, simple guidelines, and support to follow the rules. The purpose is not to stop AI, but to make sure AI is safe before it affects people’s lives.

Voters should support AI rules that protect people while still allowing new technology to grow.

Why Voters Should Support It

Voters should support the AI Transparency and Safety Act because AI is becoming part of everyday life. People deserve to know when AI is being used, how their data is being handled, and whether AI decisions are fair.

This law protects privacy, reduces misinformation, gives people the right to human review, and makes companies responsible for the AI systems they create. It does not ban AI. It simply makes sure AI is used in a way that is honest, safe, and fair.
